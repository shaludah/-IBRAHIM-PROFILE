1.PREAMBLE

We, the old students of EYN COMPREHENSIVE SECONDARY SCHOOL KWARHI; realizing that we have and share a common past and heritage, realizing that by coming together we shall be affording better means of knowing and helping to solve each other’s problems and having a firm belief that such group will contribute to our Alma mater in various ways, and in furtherance thereof of the foregoing principle; do resolve to form ourselves into an Association. In realization that the old boys of our alma mata deserve a legal and efficient association to unite and harmonize us, we provide and uphold this constitution for ourselves. This realization is in keeping with the 1999 Constitution of the Federal Republic of Nigeria, Section 37 (The Right to Peaceful Assembly and Association).

2.NAME OF ASSOCIATION

The name of the association shall be EYN CSS KWARHI OLD STUDENTS’ ASSOCIATION (ECOSSA).

3.COLOUR

The colour of the Association shall be Brown and White.

4.HEAD OFFICE/SECRETARIAT

The Head Office/Secretariat of the Association shall be situated at E.Y.N COMPRESHENSIVE SECONDARY SCHOOL, KWARHI, HONG LOCAL GOVERNMENT OF ADAMAWA STATE, provided that the Association may maintain liaison/branch offices in other cities/towns as the National Executive Committee may from time to time decide.

5.AIMS AND OBJECTIVES

The aims and objectives of the Association shall be:

  1. To keep alive among Old Boys interest in the well-being of EYN CSS KWARHI, here-in-after referred to as the “School”.
  2. To Promote, maintain and defend the integrity and good name and image of the Alma Mata, EYN COMPREHENSIVE SECONDARY SCHOOL KWARHI.
  3. Provision of moral and financial support to the SCHOOL in its educational, management and other developmental pursuits.
  4. Supporting the P.T.A, friendly bodies, clubs, branches and associations of the School, in matters that will promote positive solutions to problems of the School in general.
  5. Provision of a forum for free exchange of ideas and resources for the Progress of the Alma Mata.
  6. Promotion and Creation of good relations and spirit of fellowship among members of the association.
  7. Promotion of welfare, progress and upliftment of old students in all branches and among current students of the School.
  8. Recognition and encouragement of the old and current students of the School for exemplary achievement and dedication in various aspects of human endeavour.
  9. The Association shall be a non-political, non-ethnic and non-profit sharing body.
  10. To meet, deliberate and formulate plans and measures deemed suitable and appropriate for the advancement, betterment and progress of the students and the school.
  11. To help the school consistently maintain high academic and moral standard for which the school is renowned for.
  12. Encourage the management, staff and students of the school to maintain the ideals of the school and to offer services for the overall interest of the school.

6. MEMBERSHIP OF THE ASSOCIATION

There shall be three (3) categories of membership:

  • Full Membership,
  • Associate membership,
  • Honorary Membership.

A. Full Membership

  1. This Membership is open to all old students of EYN CSS provided such an individual has attended the school for at least one academic session. In a situation where an old student has spent less than one academic session, such an individual will be qualified for membership if he has shown proven interest in the school as specified in section 5 (3).
  2. Any person who becomes a member by virtue of section 6 (1) (a) shall be entitled to: -
    • Attend the General Assembly
    • To hold any office whatsoever,
    • To vote or be voted for at any election of the Association.

B.Associate Membership

Associate Membership shall be given to persons who did not attend the school but have worked in the school as academic and non-academic staff and have shown proven interest in the school.

C. Honorary Membership

Honorary Membership shall be given to Public-spirited individuals and institutions that have served or contributed to the development of the school in a special way. Individual citizens who have exceptionally excelled in the service of mankind, the nation and the church may have an honorary membership conferred on them by the association in recognition of such service.

Members shall have the right to associate with any branch, chapter, club or the nearest group to them provided that such a group, branch, chapter or club operates in consonance with the provisions of this constitution.

7.SUPREME AUTHORITY

The supreme authority of the association shall be exercised by the GENERAL ASSEMBLY of the Association.

8. GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF ECOSA

  1. The National Executive Committee shall convoke a reunion of all Old Students of the School which shall be called ‘‘The General Assembly’’ as necessary from time to time provided a meeting is held at least once every two years.
  2. The General Assembly shall deliberate only on matters presented before it by the National Executive Committee.
  3. All Old Students present at the General Assembly shall have a right to vote on any resolution/matter presented at the General Assembly.
  4. The General Assembly shall hold on such dates as may be determined by the National Executive Committee provided that all branches and clubs of the association shall be given a notice of 90 days of the General Assembly.
  5. The president shall Preside at the General Assembly and in the absence of the President and Vice President, the National Executive Committee members present shall among themselves appoint any member present to preside over the meeting.
  6. The General Assembly shall receive and approve the reports of the President, Secretary and Financial Secretary for the year.
  7. The General Assembly shall receive and approve the report of the audited account of the association as presented by the auditor.
  8. The quorum for the General Assembly shall be 50.
  9. In an election year, the General Assembly shall elect officers as specified in section 11.
  10. The General Assembly shall deliberate on any matters laid before it by the National Executive Committee.

9.NATIONAL EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE

  1. The National Executive Committee shall comprise of the following: -
    • all elected officers and Ex-Officio members except the Auditor as contained in section 11.
    • all branch coordinators of the Association.
    • Four (4) representatives from each set consisting of two (2) males and two (2) females.
  2. The quorum of the National Executive Committee meeting shall be Fifteen (15).
  3. In the absence of the President and Vice President, the National Executive Committee members present shall among themselves appoint any member present to preside at that meeting.
  4. Efforts shall be made to reflect, as far as possible, the different generations of students as well as the geographical spread of branches.

10.OFFICERS OF THE ASSOCIATION AND THEIR DUTIES

  1. The Officers of the Association shall be elected at the General Assembly and in accordance with the provision of this constitution.
  2. The Officers of the Association shall be: –
    • The president
    • The vice president
    • The general secretary
    • Assistant general secretary
    • Financial secretary
    • Treasurer
    • Welfare secretary
    • Public relations officer
    • Auditor

  3. 3.Qualifications to hold Office – A member of the Association shall be qualified to hold any Office if:
    • He/she is a full member of the Association and has paid, as at the date of his/her nomination, his/her membership fee and Branch Dues, as and when due, for five (5) consecutive years inclusive of the year of election.
    • The Officers must be persons with proven integrity, administrative skills and experience and with demonstrable capacity to serve without expecting or having consideration for pecuniary rewards and/or remuneration.
    • The Treasurer and the Financial Secretary must in addition to their respective fields of qualifications be persons with basic understanding and appreciation of finance and accounting.

  4. Disqualification from holding any Office – A member shall be disqualified to hold any office in the Association if during election campaigns: -
    • There is evidence that he/she is sponsored by or has received any financial assistance or inducement from any government, organizations or bodies corporate
    • He/she is associated with a cult group, sponsoring cultism or threatening other contestant or members of the association
    • Any member who has held an elective office as an officer for two (2) terms shall not be eligible to contest for a national office until at least ten (10) years after his/her last term of office.

  5. Duties of National Officers-

    1. The president

      2. The President of the Association who will be the chairman/chairperson shall:

      • Preside at all General Assembly and at other meetings including the National Executive Committee meetings for the Association.
      • Cause the summoning of meetings subject to the decision of the National Executive Committee.
      • Direct and co-ordinate the activities of the association.
      • Perform duties relating to finance.
      • Give a casting vote in case of a tie during a voting exercise.
      • Present the annual report on the activities of the Association.
      • To serve as ex-officio member of all sub-committee of the Association.
      • He shall represent and be the spokesman of the Association at all official function that requires the attendance of the Association.
      • He shall authorize all disbursement of the Association’s funds in conformity with its approved budget.
      • He shall perform other duties that may be assigned to him from time to time by the General Assembly and or the Executive council.
      • At the end of his term, He shall present a written report on his tenure of office to the General meeting.

    2. The Vice President-

      • The Vice President shall deputize for the President in his absence and preside at all meetings over which the president by constitution has power to preside.
      • He/she shall perform all other duties which the President owing to ill health or any other cause is unable to perform or as shall be ascribed to him by the President, Executive Committee or General Meeting.
      • In case of persistent absence; the Vice President shall perform the duties of the President until another President is duly elected at the next General Assembly.
      • Shall encourage and supervise the formation of new Branches within his/her zonal supervisory jurisdiction in line with the guidelines set out in this Constitution.

    3. The General Secretary-

      4. The General Secretary to the association shall:

      • Summon all meetings, He/she shall notify members of meetings on due dates as on the advice of the President and record all proceedings emanating from such meetings.
      • Handle all correspondence of the Association or the National Executive Committee.
      • Furnish members with annual or any special report of the association.
      • Perform all duties allotted to him by the President or the Executive Committee or the Annual or Extra- -ordinary General Meeting.
      • Take care of general management of the secretariat of the Association.
      • May be a signatory/ guardian to the Association account.
      • He shall keep a list, data base and communication addresses and telephone numbers of all members of the association and an up-to- date list of officers of branches of the association.

    4. d)ASSISTANT GENERAL SECRETARY-

      • The Assistant General Secretary shall assist the General Secretary in all Secretariat duties while He/she performs the duties of the General Secretary in his absence.
      • He/she shall perform all other duties as may be assigned to him/her by the President, the General Secretary, the National Executive Committee or the General Assembly.

    5. FINANCIAL SECRETARY-

      6. the financial secretary of the Association shall:

      • Be in constant touch with branches of the association so as to ensure that all members pay their annual dues as prescribed by this constitution and other dues or subscriptions as may be determined by the General Assembly or the Executive Committee.
      • To prepare from time to time the list of defaulting members.
      • To circulate such lists to the General Assembly and the National Executive Committee.
      • To collect and promptly pay all monies within 24 hours of receipt to the Treasurer as authorized by the Association.
      • Prepare before every General Meeting the audited Balance Sheet and Statement of Accounts of the Association.
      • Prepare annual budget of the association after collection and collation of all inputs from various stakeholders.

    6. TREASURER-

      7. the treasurer of the Association shall:

      • Receive all monies of the Association from the financial secretary and issue numbered receipt for money collected.
      • Deposit all monies received into the Association bank account within 48 hours.
      • Be signatory to the Association bank account in accordance with Section 16 (5)
      • Submit annually to the Auditor not later than 15th March of the following year all books, accounts and other documents required by them.
      • Present his report together with an audited account to the General Assembly.
      • On leaving the office, hand over the Association’s property in his possession to his successors or as the circumstances may dictate.

11. ELECTION OF OFFICERS

  1. There shall be established an Electoral Committee comprising of a minimum of three and a maximum of five Electoral officers, one of whom shall preside;
  2. the presiding officer shall be appointed by the Executive Committee at its first meeting held in an election year, to conduct the elections of officers.
  3. the members of the electoral committee shall declare that they have no interest in any of the offices being contested for.
  4. The procedure for the appointment of the members of the Electoral Committee, nomination of candidates for election, withdrawal of nominations and disqualification from election shall be as set out in the Schedule to this Constitution.
  5. Prior to the election, all offices shall be declared vacant.
  6. Only members who are committed up to date financially and are confirmed by their branch or club may vote or may be voted for.
  7. All offices are tenable for three years after which there shall be an election at the General Assembly.
  8. The removal of an officer shall be subject to the approval of not less than two- thirds (2/3) majority of all members present during a general meeting during which such measure is being considered, provided always that the officer so affected be granted adequate fair hearing.
